ALEXANDER
-------------------------------------------

Ingredients:
▸ 30 ml (1 oz) Cognac
▸ 30 ml (1 oz) Crème de Cacao (Brown)
▸ 30 ml (1 oz) Fresh Cream

Garnish:
Sprinkle fresh ground nutmeg on top.

Method:
Pour all ingredients into cocktail shaker
filled with ice cubes. Shake and strain
into a chilled cocktail glass.

-------------------------------------------

a splash of Soda Water. Stir gently.

BRAMBLE
-------------------------------------------

Ingredients:
▸ 50 ml (1 3/4 oz) Gin
▸ 25 ml (3/4 oz) Fresh Lemon Juice
▸ 12.5 ml (1/2 oz) Sugar Syrup
▸ 15 ml (1/2 oz) Crème de Mûre

Garnish:
Garnish optionally with a lemon slice and
blackberries.

Method:
Pour all ingredients into a cocktail shaker
except the Crème de Mûre, shake well with
ice, strain into chilled old fashioned
glass filled with crushed ice, then pour
the blackberry liqueur (Crème de Mûre) over
the top of the drink, in a circular motion.



BRANDY CRUSTA
-------------------------------------------

Ingredients:
▸ 52.5 ml (1 3/4 oz) Brandy
▸ 7.5 ml (1/4 oz) Maraschino Luxardo
▸ 1 bar spoon Curacao
▸ 15 ml (1/2 oz) Fresh Lemon Juice
▸ 1 bar spoon Simple Syrup
▸ 2 dash Aromatic Bitters

Garnish:
Rub a slice of orange (or lemon) around the
rim of the glass and dip it in pulverized
white sugar, so that the sugar will adhere
to the edge of the glass. Carefully curling
place the orange/lemon peel around the
inside of the glass.

Method:
Mix together all ingredients with ice cubes
in a mixing glass and strain into prepared
slim cocktail glass.

CANCHANCHARA
-------------------------------------------

Ingredients:
▸ 60 ml (2 oz) Cuban Aguardiente
▸ 15 ml (1/2 oz) Fresh Lime Juice
▸ 15 ml (1/2 oz) Raw Honey
▸ 50 ml (1 3/4 oz) Water

Garnish:
Lime wedge.

Method:

spread the mixture on the bottom and sides
of the glass. Add cracked ice, and then the
rum. End by energetically stirring from
bottom to top.

IRISH COFFEE
-------------------------------------------

Ingredients:
▸ 50 ml (1 3/4 oz) Irish Whiskey
▸ 120 ml (3 oz) Hot coffee
▸ 50 ml (1 3/4 oz) Fresh cream (Chilled)
▸ 1 teaspoon Sugar

Method:
Warm black coffee is poured into a
pre-heated Irish coffee glass. Whiskey and
at least one teaspoon of sugar is added and
stirred until dissolved. Fresh thick
chilled cream is carefully poured over the
back of a spoon held just above the surface
of the coffee. The layer of cream will
float on the coffee without mixing. Plain
sugar can be replaced with sugar syrup

RAMOS FIZZ
-------------------------------------------

Ingredients:
▸ 45 ml (1 1/2 oz) Gin
▸ 15 ml (1/2 oz) Fresh Lime Juice
▸ 15 ml (1/2 oz) Fresh Lemon Juice
▸ 30 ml (1 oz) Sugar Syrup
▸ 60 ml (2 oz) Cream
▸ 30 ml (1 oz) Egg white
▸ 3 dash Orange Flower Water
▸ 2 drops Vanilla Extract
▸ 1 top up Soda Water

Method:
Pour all ingredients except soda water in a
cocktail shaker with ice, Shake for two
minutes, double strain in a glass, pour the
drink back in the shaker, and hard shake
without ice for one minute. Strain into a
highball glass, top up with soda.

Note:
The drink was invented by Henry Ramos in
1888, at his bar Meyer’s Table d’Hôtel
Internationale in New Orleans. The Ramos
Fizz was originally shaken for 12 minutes
by a crew of 30 bartenders who passed the
shaker from one to another.

SAZERAC
-------------------------------------------

Ingredients:
▸ 50 ml (1 3/4 oz) Cognac
▸ 10 ml (1/4 oz) Absinthe
▸ 1 piece Sugar Cube
▸ 2 dash Peychaud’s Bitters

Garnish:
Garnish with lemon zest.

Method:
Rinse a chilled old-fashioned glass with
the absinthe, add crushed ice and set it
aside. Stir the remaining ingredients over
ice in a mixing glass. Discard the ice and
any excess absinthe from the prepared
glass, strain the mixed drink into the
glass.

Note:
The original recipe changed after the
American Civil War, Rye Whiskey substituted
Cognac as it became hard to obtain.
